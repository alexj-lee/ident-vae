Identifiability and parameterization in VAEs

Principles of VAEs

largely from the paper Variational autoencoders and nonlinear ICA, (Khemakhem, 2020)


In general, we hope to model some random vectors $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^d$ by using a latent variable $\mathbf{z} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ to factorize the joint model: $$ p_\mathbf{\theta}(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{z}) = p_\mathbf{\theta}(x \mid \mathbf{z})p_\mathbf{\theta}(\mathbf{z}) $$

we typically model the conditional density with the decoder of an encoder-decoder neural network with vector parameters $\mathbf{\theta} \in \Theta$
($p_\mathbf{\theta}(\mathbf{z})$ is a prior over the latents)

The model then gives the observed distribution as:
$p_\theta(x) = \int p_{\mathbf{\theta}}(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{z}) \mathrm{d}\mathbf{z}$

We assume there is a true data generating process given by $p_{\mathbf{\theta^*}}\ (\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{z}) = p_{\theta^*} (\mathbf{x} \mid \mathbf{z})$ $ p_{\mathbf{\theta}^*}\ (\mathbf{z}^{*})$, with $\mathbf{\theta}^*$ as true but unknown parameters.


We collect observations of $\mathbf{x}$: $$ \mathcal{D} = \{\mathbf{x}^{(1)}, ..., \mathbf{x}^{(\mathit{N})}\} \newline$$ $$ \mathbf{z}^{*(\mathit{i})} \sim p_{\mathbf{\theta}^*}\ (\mathbf{z}) \\ \mathbf{x}^{(\mathit{i})} \sim p_{\mathbf{\theta}^*}\ (\mathbf{x} \mid \mathbf{z}^{*(\mathit{i})}) $$



and optimize $\mathbf{\theta}$ towards the likelihood objective in order to estimate:

$p_{\theta}(\mathbf{x}) \approx p_{\theta^*}(\mathbf{x})$

In the end, we learn a full generative model:
$p_\mathbf{\theta}(\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{z}) = p_\mathbf{\theta}(\mathbf{x}\mid\mathbf{z})p_\mathbf{\theta}(\mathbf{z})$ AND
an inference model $q_\phi({\mathbf{z}\mid{\mathbf{x}}})$

But really, the only thing we can be certain of is that $p_\mathbf{\theta^*}(\mathbf{x})$ is meaningful
